Direct answer — what happened this week:
Multiple major natural disasters occurred worldwide this week: the most destructive were Typhoon Kalmaegi (called TinoPH) producing catastrophic flooding and a rising death toll across the central Philippines and then making landfall in Vietnam; a powerful tornado that devastated Rio Bonito do Iguaçu in Paraná, Brazil; and a cluster of severe storms (tornadoes, giant hail, and flash floods) affecting regions from Brazil and Paraguay to Indonesia, South Africa, Spain and China. Several volcanic, seismic, avalanche and landslide incidents were also reported.

Highly significant events (each summarized in one paragraph):
- Typhoon Kalmaegi (TinoPH) — Philippines and Vietnam:  A major tropical cyclone caused catastrophic flooding across central Philippines (notably Cebu and other provinces), with repeatedly updated and escalating casualty reports as rescue operations continued. Local officials and news updates reported dozens to hundreds of deaths in different updates (examples: reports of at least 26, 48, 92, 111, 114, 181 and higher in successive tweets), entire communities submerged, thousands displaced, and a government-declared state of calamity in some areas. The cyclone then moved toward and made landfall in Vietnam with winds reported up to ~205 km/h and ongoing risks of storm surge, mudslides and flash flooding ([multiple updates and impacts in the Philippines](https://x.com/Volcaholic1/status/1985695617687896143), [landfall in Vietnam and campaign warning](https://x.com/Volcaholic1/status/1986399536613412936), [one casualty update showing 114 deaths](https://x.com/Volcaholic1/status/1986433891331457056)). This event drove large-scale rescue operations and repeated appeals for assistance.

- Rio Bonito do Iguaçu, Paraná, Brazil — destructive tornado:  A powerful tornado (classified by meteorologists as at least F2 and possibly F3) tore through Rio Bonito do Iguaçu, causing extensive destruction, winds reported up to ~250 km/h, at least five fatalities, and more than 430 injured. Authorities described up to ~80% of the urban area as severely damaged; widespread power and water outages followed and emergency teams (including field hospitals) were deployed for search-and-rescue and medical care ([initial report and details](https://x.com/Volcaholic1/status/1987129623239393737); [aerial aftermath imagery](https://x.com/Volcaholic1/status/1987132020154065234)).

Other notable/discrete events and developments:
