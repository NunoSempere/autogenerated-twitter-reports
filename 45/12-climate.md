Direct answer — what happened last week: The week’s coverage was dominated by the opening of COP30-related events in Belém, Brazil and a flurry of UN and science reports warning current national plans and finance flows are far short of what’s needed to avoid dangerous warming and to protect people from climate impacts. At the same time there were high-profile policy announcements (Brazil’s Tropical Forest Forever fund), renewed focus on adaptation finance and agrifood-system solutions, more evidence of accelerating renewables growth, and reminders from extreme-weather events and cryosphere science that impacts are intensifying now.

Major developments (each in one paragraph):
- COP30 opening, leaders and forest fund: World leaders and UN officials convened in Belém ahead of COP30, with the UN secretary-general stressing speed and scale on clean energy (see @UNFCCC live message: https://x.com/UNFCCC/status/1986780714256253313) and a high-profile launch of Brazil’s “Tropical Forest Forever” facility — a major new forest-finance initiative touted as a cornerstone of the summit (coverage and analysis: https://x.com/CarbonBrief/status/1986713735394648516; reactions/launch coverage: https://x.com/FAOclimate/status/1986806590708838843). Carbon Brief and others rolled out COP30 trackers and Q&As to follow negotiating text, the global goal on adaptation, and other agenda items (see interactive tracking: https://x.com/CarbonBrief/status/1987076123268722905).

- UN/UNEP warnings on pledges and warming: UNEP-related coverage and analysis emphasized that new country plans and current policies still “barely move the needle” on projected warming — recent summaries warned the world remains on a trajectory well above 1.5°C (coverage and reads: https://x.com/CarbonBrief/status/1986996605468262481; broader briefing: https://x.com/CarbonBrief/status/1986848050187280737). Alongside this were repeated references to UN graphics showing a persistent finance shortfall for adaptation.

- Adaptation finance and tracking the global goal on adaptation: A major theme was the adaptation finance gap and efforts at COP30 to finally agree robust ways to track progress on the Global Goal on Adaptation. Carbon Brief and UNEP explainers/charts on the adaptation finance shortfall were widely shared (e.g. finance-gap charts: https://x.com/CarbonBrief/status/1986838058533081540; Q&A on tracking adaptation: https://x.com/CarbonBrief/status/1986749975133372755). FAO pushed agrifood-system finance and programs (SCALA, FAST) as essential to adaptation and food security (examples: https://x.com/FAOclimate/status/1987143095566147653; https://x.com/FAOclimate/status/1986374265818513637).

Other notable developments and themes:
- Science and risk signals: Cryosphere and sea-level science featured strongly — an interdisciplinary State of the Cryosphere report warned about accelerating ice melt and risks to ocean circulation (AMOC) (https://x.com/rahmstorf/status/1986565338796896655). Carbon Brief highlighted modelling showing that rapid emissions cuts would materially reduce “locked-in” sea-level rise by 2300 (avoid ~64 cm) (https://x.com/CarbonBrief/status/1986113408035426753).

- Extreme weather and impacts: Severe weather underscored urgency — catastrophic flooding from Typhoon Tino was highlighted (https://x.com/ClimateReality/status/1986873583771980141), analysis and discussion of record storm behaviour in 2025 and what that implies for the future were promoted (https://x.com/ClimateCentral/status/1986882004399370378), and regional temperature extremes (e.g. an unusually warm first week of November in the UK) were flagged by scientists (https://x.com/PGDynes/status/1986934271240446464).

- Energy and technology trends: Multiple threads showed renewables continuing to accelerate (solar described as explosive/exponential growth and fastest-growing energy technology in history: https://x.com/RARohde/status/1985850757212410301; renewable capacity additions growth noted via Ember retweet: https://x.com/dwallacewells/status/1986904100324163971). At the same time, coverage flagged ongoing fossil-industry influence (reports that 5,000+ fossil lobbyists attended the last four COPs: https://x.com/ClimateReality/status/1986946868547366938) and pollution/permit problems around US LNG terminals (https://x.com/ClimateReality/status/1985790457264177522).

- Policy, courts and politics: US federal-court litigation tracking (nearly 40 suits) around administration rollbacks continued to be covered (https://x.com/insideclimate/status/1986939420415828172). Debates about prominent private-sector climate commentary (Bill Gates’ memo and the scientific pushback) stayed in public discussion (example thread of responses and critique widely shared via scientists such as @MichaelEMann).

- Food, land and nature focus: FAO and others pushed agrifood-systems solutions at COP30 — promoting climate-resilient crops (e.g., drought- and flood-tolerant beans in Colombia: https://x.com/FAOclimate/status/1987143095566147653), guidance on agrifood climate risk management (SCALA: https://x.com/FAOclimate/status/1986796138440151319), and attention to nature/forests with data showing global deforestation trends (https://x.com/CarbonBrief/status/1986526000457322573).

Key patterns and trends to watch:
- Persistent gap between pledges/policies and warming outcomes: Multiple UN/analysis pieces repeated that current plans still point to well above 1.5°C if not strengthened. COP30 will be judged on whether it closes any of those gaps.
- Fast technical progress but slow political/economic reallocation: Renewable deployment is surging, creating potential for a rapid transition, but fossil infrastructure and finance still dominate and fossil lobbying remains influential.
- Adaptation and finance are moving to the center of talks: Pressure to close the adaptation finance gap and to agree strong tracking metrics for the Global Goal on Adaptation is a central theme.
- Nature/land/agriculture rising in prominence: Forest finance (e.g., Tropical Forest Forever), agrifood-system programmes, and soil/land health discussions are front-and-center at COP30.

Immediate outlook/what to watch this week: COP30 negotiating outcomes — especially any concrete commitments on adaptation finance, clarity on tracking the Global Goal on Adaptation, implementation details (and funding) for the Tropical Forest Forever facility, and any strengthened NDC/near-term emissions commitments. Also watch whether science and finance reports galvanize new pledges or concrete finance announcements during summit events.
