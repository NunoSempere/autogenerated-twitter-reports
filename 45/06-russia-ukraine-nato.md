Direct answer — what happened and NATO/partner responses:

- Russia launched a massive overnight strike campaign that used hundreds of attack drones and dozens of missiles to hit Ukrainian cities and critical infrastructure. Ukraine and partners report roughly 450 strike drones and 45 missiles used in the attack; Ukraine’s air defenses and mobile units reportedly neutralized the vast majority of the drones (Zelenskyy: ~406/458 drones shot down; other reporting: 450+ drones and 45 missiles) and shot down some missiles, but strikes caused civilian casualties, building collapses, fires, and extensive power outages across multiple regions including Dnipro, Kharkiv, Kyiv, Poltava and others ([ZelenskyyUa](https://x.com/ZelenskyyUa/status/1987071260082216970); [NOELreports](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987078282383126548); [NOELreports Dnipro strike](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987078938649059636)).

Key themes and topics related to the question:

- Mass drone warfare and strikes on energy/civilian infrastructure: Moscow’s recent tactic has focused on energy, gas and urban targets to cause blackouts and civilian disruption ahead of winter; Ukrainian leaders and Air Force spokespeople framed many of these attacks as deliberate terrorism against civilian life and infrastructure ([NOELreports — energy focus](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987137178502054366); [ZelenskyyUa](https://x.com/ZelenskyyUa/status/1987071260082216970)).

- Ukraine’s growing unmanned/drone capabilities and counter‑drone measures: Ukraine is scaling domestic Mavic-style production and mass-producing strike drones, expanding unmanned air-defense capability (appointment of a Commander of Unmanned Air Defence Systems), and fielding indigenous and allied low-cost AD systems (British Raven, STING etc.) to intercept cruise missiles and Shahed drones ([ZelenskyyUa — appointment](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987136447141355639); [wartranslated / Raven intercepts](https://x.com/wartranslated/status/1987128327916958083); [EuromaidanPress drone mass‑prod.](https://x.com/EuromaidanPress/status/1986933686873305311)).

- Ukrainian strikes deeper into Russian-held/ Russian territory and Crimea: Ukraine continues to hit infrastructure and military targets inside Russia and occupied Crimea — substations, fuel depots, and even an S-400 TEL and ammo depot in Crimea credited to SOF/SSO operations — demonstrating increased Ukrainian deep-strike and SOF reach ([Gerashchenko_en S-400/Cimea](https://x.com/Gerashchenko_en/status/1987116861469643158); [Osinttechnical footage](https://x.com/Osinttechnical/status/1987097347638059063); [wartranslated — substations hit](https://x.com/wartranslated/status/1987094698998014021)).

- Allied hardware and procurement debates: Ukraine is seeking more long-range air‑defense and strike systems (Patriot requests; Tomahawk discussion pending U.S. presidential approval), allies are delivering and developing systems (Sweden funding long‑range strike drones; UK Raven system successes; UK Ajax deliveries to NATO eastern flank), and the United States and some NATO partners are stepping up material support while others push for political or legal solutions to fund Ukraine’s needs ([NOELreports — Patriots/Tomahawks](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987138672035004881); [Mylovanov on Ajax](https://x.com/Mylovanov/status/1987143704247755205); [NOELreports — Sweden drones](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1986818821710037277); [Osinttechnical Raven footage](https://x.com/Osinttechnical/status/1986930861963673939)).

- Political divergence within NATO/EU and bilateral politics: Allies are split on methods and policy. The U.S. has publicly backed using frozen Russian assets as a funding tool for Ukraine, but the plan faces legal and political resistance in Europe (Belgium concerns, Slovakia/Slovak PM Fico opposing), and Hungary secured a U.S. one‑year exemption to keep importing Russian oil—moves that complicate unified EU/NATO responses ([Reuters / US backs frozen assets](https://x.com/Reuters/status/1986937989402861599); [NOELreports — Belgium/asset issues](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987138498797658354); [wartranslated / Hungary exemption](https://x.com/wartranslated/status/1987110740684111956); [NOELreports on Hungary exemption](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987069618813329773)).

Notable patterns and trends relevant to the question:

- Escalation of drone-led campaigns: Both sides increasingly rely on swarms (Russia: mass Shahed/loitering drones; Ukraine: FPV and guided strike drones). This has shifted the fight toward attrition, low-cost unmanned strikes, and rapid countermeasure development (interceptor drones, EW, low-cost SAMs) ([Gerashchenko_en mass attack summary](https://x.com/Gerashchenko_en/status/1987083504681705649); [bayraktar_1love STING footage](https://x.com/bayraktar_1love/status/1986795222961918040)).

- Strategic focus on energy and logistics: Russia’s recent wave targeted Ukraine’s energy and transport hubs to inflict civilian hardship; Ukraine responded with strikes on Russian power substations and fuel depots — both sides increasingly attack the other’s logistics, energy and economic infrastructure ([NOELreports — energy outages](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987069859675709899); [wartranslated Russian grid strikes](https://x.com/wartranslated/status/1987094698998014021); [EuromaidanPress on Russian nodes hit](https://x.com/EuromaidanPress/status/1986941242404417561)).

- Political fragmentation among NATO/EU partners: There is growing divergence (e.g., Hungary’s exemption and insistence on different approach; Belgium’s legal reservations over frozen asset use; Slovakia’s PM opposition) that complicates a single unified EU/NATO approach to funding and sanctions, even as many NATO members expand military aid ([NOELreports on Fico](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987160441026490812); [NOELreports on Belgium/EU assets](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987134718848954721); [wartranslated on Hungary exemption](https://x.com/wartranslated/status/1987110740684111956)).

- NATO deterrence posture and warning signals: NATO allies are conducting nuclear drills and exercises and senior NATO/European officials have publicly warned about Russia’s potential to threaten NATO territory; military planners are rehearsing defensive scenarios beyond Ukraine’s borders ([Reuters — Rutte on nuclear drills](https://x.com/Reuters/status/1987144352229945385); [Reuters / German general warning](https://x.com/Reuters/status/1986780946557780051); [POLITICOEurope coverage](https://x.com/POLITICOEurope/status/1986817727814607029)).

Significant events/developments (short focused paragraphs):

1) Massive overnight drone + missile strike on Ukraine and Ukraine’s air‑defense response:
Russia launched a large coordinated strike (reported ~450 attack drones + 45 missiles) that hit multiple Ukrainian regions, targeted energy infrastructure and residential areas, and caused deaths, injuries and widespread blackouts; Ukraine reports a high interception rate (hundreds of drones shot down), but significant damage and humanitarian impacts remain. These attacks further underline how drone swarm tactics and strikes on civilian infrastructure have become central features of the current campaign ([ZelenskyyUa confirmation and appeals for more air defense](https://x.com/ZelenskyyUa/status/1987071260082216970); [NOELreports on interceptions and impacts](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987078282383126548)).

2) Ukraine’s deep strikes into Russian territory and SOF strikes in Crimea (S‑400 and ammo depot):
Ukrainian Special Operations / deep-strike units have claimed successful strikes on Russian military assets in Crimea (destruction of an S-400 TEL and an ammunition depot) and multiple attacks on Russian substations and fuel depots across Russian regions and occupied territory, showing an ongoing ability to project force deep into enemy logistics hubs and degrade Russia’s rear infrastructure ([Gerashchenko_en on Crimea S‑400](https://x.com/Gerashchenko_en/status/1987116861469643158); [Osinttechnical FP‑2 footage](https://x.com/Osinttechnical/status/1987097347638059063); [wartranslated on Russian substations](https://x.com/wartranslated/status/1987094698998014021)).

3) Political split over frozen Russian assets, sanctions and energy exemptions affecting NATO/EU cohesion:
The U.S. has publicly backed using frozen Russian assets to fund Ukraine, but EU progress is stalled by legal and political objections (notably in Belgium), while Hungary obtained a U.S. exemption to keep importing Russian oil for a year and Slovakia’s PM publicly opposed the EU frozen-assets plan. These fractures complicate a unified Western financial/political strategy even as military aid continues to flow from many partners ([Reuters / US backs frozen assets](https://x.com/Reuters/status/1986937989402861599); [NOELreports on Belgium and frozen assets](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987134718848954721); [wartranslated / Hungary exemption](https://x.com/wartranslated/status/1987110740684111956); [NOELreports on Fico](https://x.com/NOELreports/status/1987160441026490812)).

Bottom line / implications for NATO countries’ actions: NATO members and partners are increasing military support (AD systems, strike drones, logistics and R&D) while simultaneously facing political friction over sanctions, energy exemptions and funding mechanisms. NATO officials are also publicly signaling increased deterrence preparedness. The war’s character continues to be dominated by massed drone attacks, counter‑drone innovations, and reciprocal strikes on energy/logistics networks — driving Ukraine’s urgent requests for more long‑range air defenses and strike options and pushing partners to balance military aid with fractured geopolitical and legal calculations.
